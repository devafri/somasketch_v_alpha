Soma: the greek word, meaning "body"

Somatization: Physical symptoms of mental or emotional distress

Mental illness is NOT all in your head. Many experience physical symptoms without noticing the cognitive and emotional symptoms that are used most commonly for diagnosis. Today, the average time between diagnosis and treatment is 11 years. More than a decade. We are creating tools that focus on the somatic (less stigma-laden) symptoms of mental illness in hopes of making diagnosis and communication easier and more accessible than ever.

The Somatic Journal App

Somatic Journaling can be used to prepare for somatic intervention, or just to build a foundation for mindfulness and emotional stability. By observing bodily sensations at the same time as naming feelings patients can learn to better connect their emotional and physical experiences, reducing symptom anxiety and dissociation. Gratitude helps interrupt any associated negative cognitions which arise during the exercise.

Soma Sketch provides a well balanced and minimally invasive exercise for emotional health.

Easy to get started

Because our approach is based on the lived experience of patients in therapy, kaizen principles, as well as the latest research, clients need only a small amount of time and effort to start tracking and addressing their symptoms. No lengthy psycho ed or jargon-filled worksheets, just 5 minutes of effort daily.

How it works

DRAW

Draw how your body feels:

Did you know that emotions can wreak havoc in your body, even if you are not aware of them?! Through a process called somatization buried psychological or emotional distress can cause headaches, unexplained numbness, chiropractic issues, irritable bowels, and much more. Depression and anxiety are real medical illnesses that can show up as physical symptoms, especially in the strongest among us.

Paying attention to bodily sensations not only stops thought spirals and dissociation by getting you out of your head (and into), it is the first step towards emotional awareness and wellness!

WRITE

Write a list of your feelings:

Journaling is like meditation on paper; it promotes self awareness, which is an essential leadership quality. We use a very easy and powerful technique to get you started. Listing your feelings helps with managing your emotions: "...naming our emotions tends to diffuse their charge and lessen the burden they create. The psychologist Dan Siegel refers to this practice as 'name it to tame it.'” - Dan Siegel, As Quoted by NY Times article

REFLECT

Reflect on 3 positive things:

Spending just a moment being thankful daily, even for the smallest things, rewires your brain for happiness: "gratitude causes synchronized activation in multiple brain regions, and lights up parts of the brain’s reward pathways and the hypothalamus. In short, just like Prozac, gratitude can boost neurotransmitter serotonin and activate the brain stem to produce dopamine." - Thrive Global, NIH

Our Story

Felix and Sasha, the cofounders of Soma Sketch, come from two opposite sides of the healthcare industry. Felix was investing in health startups and managing hospital finances while Sasha was transforming customer experiences professionally and navigating the pitfalls of the mental health system personally. After quitting their fancy, but draining, corporate jobs they decided to work on something they actually care about, and to do it on their terms. They saw the need for more intuitive mental health tools that align with the reality of living with mental illness (as the 1 in 5 humans around the world do), while still incorporating the latest scientific knowledge.

We are proudly an Antler portfolio company, seeking like-minded Angels who want to transform the future of mental health care.
